Ham Sandwhich

A sandwich is a versatile and convenient meal made by placing a filling between two slices of bread. The bread can vary widely—classic white or whole grain, crusty baguettes, soft rolls, or even toasted varieties. What makes a sandwich exciting is the endless choice of fillings, which can be customized to suit any taste.

Ingredients:

  • 12 standard or 24 small soft white roll slab
  • 1/3 cup American mustard
  • 350g/ 12 oz ham, shaved or thinly sliced
  • 250g/ 8 oz Swiss cheese or other melting cheese slices
  • 100 g (7 tbsp) unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp American mustard
  • 1 tbsp poppyseeds
  • 1 tsp garlic powder (sub fresh garlic, finely minced)
  • 1/2 tsp cooking / kosher salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C / 350°F (160°C fan-forced). Line the base of a 23 x 33cm / 13 x 9" pan with paper
  2. Mix ingredients together to make butter.
  3. Cut the slab of rolls in half horizontally. Spread base and lid with the 1/3 cup mustard. Place bread in pan, top with ham then cheese.
  4. Bake 10 minutes until the cheese is melted. Remove bread from oven. Top with bread lid, pour/spoon over butter, leaving no patch naked.
  5. Cover with foil, bake 20 minutes.
  6. Remove foil. Pierce the intersections where the corners of the rolls meet to encourage pooled butter to soak into the bread.
  7. Bake uncovered for 10 minutes or until top is crusty.
  8. Cut while hot then serve and enjoy!
