Homemade Sushi

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish that consists of vinegared rice, typically paired with seafood, vegetables, and other ingredients. It is known for its delicate presentation and balance of flavors, combining the umami richness of fish or other fillings with the subtle acidity of the seasoned rice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ⅓ cups water
  • ⅔ cup uncooked short-grain white rice
  • 3 tbsps of rice vinegar
  • 3 tbps of white sugar
  • 1 1/2 tspns of of salt
  • 4 sheets of nori seaweed sheets
  • ½ pound of imitation crabmeat, flaked
  • 1 avocado - peeled, pitted, and sliced
  • 2 tbspns of pickled ginger

Steps:

  1. Gather all ingredients. Preheat the oven to 300 degrees F (150 degrees C).
  2. Bring water to a boil in a medium pot; stir in rice. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until rice is tender and water has been absorbed, 20 to 25 minutes.
  3. Mix rice vinegar, sugar, and salt in a small bowl. Gently stir into cooked rice in the pot and set aside. Lay nori sheets on a baking sheet.
  4. Heat nori in the preheated oven until warm, 1 to 2 minutes.
  5. Center 1 nori sheet on a bamboo sushi mat. Use wet hands to spread a thin layer of rice on top. Arrange 1/4 of the crabmeat, avocado, cucumber, and pickled ginger over rice in a line down the center.
  6. Lift one end of the mat and roll it tightly over filling to make a complete roll. Repeat with remaining ingredients.
  7. Use a wet, sharp knife to cut each roll into 4 to 6 slices.
  8. Serve on a plate and enjoy!
