Pasta Bolognese

Pasta Bolognese is a rich and hearty Italian dish that features pasta, typically spaghetti or tagliatelle, served with a thick meat-based sauce known as ragù alla bolognese. The sauce is a slow-cooked mixture of finely chopped or minced meat (often a combination of beef and pork), sautéed vegetables like onions, carrots, and celery, and tomatoes.

Ingredients:

  • 500g pack lean minced beef (5.1% fat)
  • A handful sliced mushrooms
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 3 tbsp sundried tomato purée
  • 400g can chopped tomato
  • A glass of red wine or 150ml/¼pt beef stock
  • 1 tsp dried mixed herb
  • ]400g penne
  • 200ml beef stock
  • A handful basil leaves, torn, to serve

Steps:

  1. Brown the beef in a large frying pan for 5 mins, then throw in the mushrooms and soften for 3 mins.
  2. Stir in the garlic and sun-dried tomato purée and cook for 2 mins.
  3. Add the tomatoes, wine or stock, dried herbs and seasoning.
  4. Bring to the boil, then simmer for 10 mins.
  5. While the sauce is simmering, cook the pasta as per pack instructions, drain, then serve with the sauce and torn basil leaves scattered on top.
  6. Serve the sauce over the pasta and enjoy!
